# Customer Validation

The 5-prospect kill-test pattern. Used before any building begins, and again
before any feature ships.

## Why 5 prospects, not 1 or 50

- 1 prospect: not a pattern; could be your friend being polite
- 5 prospects: small enough to do quickly, large enough to spot patterns
- 50 prospects: overkill for an initial check; you don't have that capacity
  yet

If 4 out of 5 give you a clear signal in either direction, you have your
answer. If the signal is mixed across 5, you have a different (also useful)
answer: the idea isn't crisp enough yet.

## How to find 5 prospects

Hard part. Not friends, not investors, not fellow founders, not anyone with
incentive to be polite.

Specifically:
- Actual people with the pain you're solving
- Budget to spend money on a solution
- Reachable within 1-2 weeks (not "we'll get to them")

Sources:
- LinkedIn warm intros (not just connections; people who'll actually talk)
- Communities where these prospects hang out (subreddits, Discord, Slack
  groups, industry forums)
- Cold email with a specific ask
- Customer-of-customer (if you've sold something else, ask those buyers
  who they know)

If you can't name 5 specific people in the first hour of trying, your idea
probably isn't targeted enough yet. Sharpen the customer profile.

## The conversation script

15-20 minute call, not a long interview.

### Opening (1 min)
> "I'm exploring [problem]. I'd love 15 minutes to understand how you
> currently handle this. I'm not selling anything; just trying to learn."

### Discovery (10 min)
- "Walk me through how you handle [problem] today."
- "What's the worst part of that?"
- "How much time/money does this cost you?"
- "What have you tried? What worked or didn't?"

LISTEN. Do not pitch your solution yet. The goal is to understand their
existing pain, not to validate yours.

### Pitch (3 min)
> "Here's what I'm thinking about building: [one paragraph]."

### Validation question (2 min)
> "If this existed and worked as described, would you pay $[X] / month for it?"

The price matters. Don't just ask "would this be useful." That always gets
a polite yes. Ask the willingness-to-pay question explicitly.

### Close (1 min)
> "Can I follow up in 4-6 weeks when I have something to show?"

## How to score the answers

For each prospect, write down:
- Their actual current solution and what they pay (in time or money)
- Their pain rating (your subjective 1-10)
- Their willingness-to-pay answer (yes / hedge / no)
- Their willingness to be a beta user (yes / hedge / no)

After 5 calls, look at the pattern:

| Pattern | What it means |
|---|---|
| 4-5 strong yes ($X paid, willing to beta) | Proceed; you have a real signal |
| 2-3 yes, 2-3 hedge | Idea has merit but not crisp; sharpen target customer or value prop |
| 0-1 yes, 4-5 hedge or no | Kill or pivot. Don't proceed with vague signal. |
| Wide variance (depends on what each said) | Customer profile too broad; you're aiming at multiple personas |

## Anti-patterns this catches

- "Customers might want it" — replaced by specific evidence
- Friends-and-family bias — by selecting non-friends
- Solution-first thinking — by listening before pitching
- Vague enthusiasm — by asking willingness-to-pay specifically
- Domain expert blindspot — by talking to other domain experts who think
  differently from you

## When to repeat

- Before initial build (the canonical "should I start" version)
- Before any major feature (modified version: "would you use THIS specific
  feature, would you pay an upgrade for it")
- After a pivot (re-validate the new direction with new prospects)
- When sales conversion drops (the pattern you're hearing might have
  shifted)

## Honest limits

- 5 prospects is small. The signal is directional, not statistical.
- People predict their behavior badly. "Yes I'd pay $X" doesn't always
  translate to actual purchase.
- The validation answers your "should I start" question, not your "will I
  succeed" question.
- If you're terrible at running the conversation, the data is bad
  regardless of prospect count. Practice on the first 1-2 calls and
  discount their data accordingly.
